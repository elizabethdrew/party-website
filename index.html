A Celestial Midsummer Night's Dream

As the summer sky begins to glow with stars, you are warmly invited to a night of magic, mischief, and moonlit celebration on the Cornish coast.

On the evening of Saturday 22nd August 2026, we will come together at Sea Swept Farm, near Mousehole, where the sea air meets open skies and the horizon stretches into twilight. As dusk falls, the farm will transform into a whimsical midsummer dream — a place of lantern light, music, dancing, and celestial wonder.

Guests are warmly invited to stay a while: camping will be available on the farm from Friday through to Monday, with plenty of time to explore the coves, coast paths, and hidden corners of this beautiful part of Cornwall during the day.

The evening itself will be playful and enchanted — and you are wholeheartedly encouraged to embrace the spirit of the night. Think starlight, fair folk, shimmering fabrics, wild crowns, and whatever magic you feel like bringing with you.

Come ready for celebration, for adventure, and for a night beneath the stars we won’t soon forget.

Accommodation

Camping

Camping is available on the farm. There is one indoor shower that all camping guests will share. There will be toilets.

Hotel, B&B, and AirB&B

There are plenty of options in the local villages, however they get booked up quickly during the summer so don't wait.

Stay In Paul Village

Paul is the nearest village to the farm being roughly a 15 mnin walk.

Stay In Mousehole

Mousehole is slightly bigger and between 15 to 30 minute walk (depending if you go by road, or brave a hidden footpath through the undergrowth).

Stay In Newlyn

You'll need to pre-book a taxi to get back to your accomodation, but there are regular buses that pick up in Newlyn and drop you at the hamlet of Sheffield (5 min walk to the farm)

Stay In Penzance

You'll need to pre-book a taxi to get back to your accomodation, but there are buses that drop you at the hamlet of Sheffield (5 min walk to the farm).
